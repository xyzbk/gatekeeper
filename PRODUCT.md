# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Gatekeeper serves maintainers, contributors, and platform engineers working in mature repositories through Codex. They need to decide whether a technically valid change fits the project while moving quickly enough to review, remediate, and re-review without reconstructing repository history by hand.

## Product Purpose

Gatekeeper is a local-first evidence workspace for repository review. It combines deterministic policy checks with durable Project Memory, then makes the verdict, authority, historical chain, and next corrective action inspectable. Success means a user can understand why a change belongs or conflicts, trace every claim to bounded evidence, prepare an aligned alternative, and verify the result in a second review.

## Positioning

The code can be correct while the decision is wrong. Gatekeeper knows the difference and shows the evidence.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and trustworthy. The voice is concise and formal without becoming ceremonial. The interface borrows the quiet utility and dark graphite restraint of OpenAI product surfaces without copying trademarks, proprietary assets, or exact layouts.

## Anti-references

Gatekeeper must not resemble a generic AI analytics dashboard, a chat clone, a glowing cyber-security console, or a marketing-card grid. Avoid decorative charts, invented metrics, purple gradients, glass effects, excessive pills, oversized radii, playful display typography, and motion that does not communicate review state.

## Design Principles

1. Show the decision chain, not just the verdict.
2. Make source authority, uncertainty, and current status explicit.
3. Use real persisted state instead of theatrical progress or placeholder data.
4. Keep local privacy and bounded evidence visible in the interaction model.
5. Complete the loop from finding to remediation to an honestly improved re-review.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, semantics, focus visibility, keyboard operation, and status announcements. Respect reduced motion, do not encode verdict or authority by color alone, keep excerpts readable as plain text, and preserve the full workflow at laptop and narrow desktop widths.
